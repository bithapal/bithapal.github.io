About me

Thanks for stopping by! I am Bipana Thapaliya, 4th year PhD student at Texas Tech University specializing in IoT security and machine learning. I work on Physical Unclonable Functions, their silicon experimentation and machine learning based vulnerability study. I am also working on data mining and big data by enhancing clustering techniques for categorical data. Under machine learning and AI, I have worked on neural networks, reinforcement learning, supervised and unsupervised learning, outlier detection, pattern recognition etc. using libraries such as Tensorflow, Keras, PyTorch, Numpy, Scipy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn etc. I also have some data mining skills such as feature engineering, data cleansing, modeling, mining and statistical analysis of data. I can write using the following programming languages: Python, C, C++, Java, C#, PHP. While I am a PhD researcher following my passion, I have also not missed the opportunity to inspire and motivate other aspiring students to follow their dreams via different roles like TA, instructor or tutor. In my free time, I like to travel and explore the outside. I am also fond of dancing and public speaking.
